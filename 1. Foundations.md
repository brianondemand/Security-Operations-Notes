### 1.1 The Role of a SOC

A **Security Operations Center (SOC)** is a centralized function that monitors, detects, analyzes, and responds to cybersecurity incidents using a combination of technology, processes, and people. It is the heart of an organization's defensive capabilities.

### 1.2 SOC Operating Models & Tiers

SOC analysts are typically organized into tiers to manage workload and escalate complex issues:
- **Tier 1 Analyst (Triage):** Monitors dashboards, triages alerts, and manages security tickets.
- **Tier 2 Analyst (Responder):** Conducts deeper investigations, hunts for threats, and manages security tools.
- **Tier 3 Analyst (Hunter):** Performs advanced threat hunting, malware reverse engineering, and leads incident response.

### 1.3 Cybersecurity Operations Lifecycle

The continuous process of defending an organization:
1.  **Detection:** Identifying potential security events.
2.  **Analysis:** Investigating events to confirm if they are incidents.
3.  **Response:** Containing, eradicating, and recovering from the incident.
4.  **Recovery:** Returning to normal operations and applying lessons learned.

### 1.4 Key Defense Frameworks

- **[[NIST Cybersecurity Framework]] (CSF):** A policy framework of standards, guidelines, and best practices to manage cybersecurity risk. Core functions: Identify, Protect, Detect, Respond, Recover.
  
- **[[MITRE ATT&CK]]:** A globally-accessible knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations. It is used as a foundation for threat modeling and detection development.
  
- **[[Cyber Kill Chain]]:** A framework developed by Lockheed Martin for understanding the stages of a cyberattack, from reconnaissance to actions on objectives.

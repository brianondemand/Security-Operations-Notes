### 2.1 Principles of Network Security Monitoring (NSM)

NSM is the collection, analysis, and escalation of indications and warnings to detect and respond to intrusions. It relies on collecting data like flow records, full packet captures (PCAP), and alerts.

### 2.2 Network Traffic Analysis

A deep dive into TCP/IP protocols is essential. Analysts must understand the normal behavior of protocols like HTTP, DNS, and SMB to spot anomalies, such as:

- **Port Scans:** Rapid connection attempts to multiple ports from a single source.
- **Data Exfiltration:** Large, unexpected outbound data transfers.
- **Command & Control (C2):** Regular beaconing to suspicious external IPs.

### 2.3 Tools of the Trade

- **PCAP Analysis:** Using tools like `tcpdump` or [[Wireshark]] to examine the raw details of network conversations.
  
- **IDS/IPS (Intrusion Detection/Prevention Systems):** Network security appliances that monitor for malicious activity. **[[Lab 4 Installing & Configuring Snort]]** is a leading open-source IDS/IPS that uses rules to detect threats.

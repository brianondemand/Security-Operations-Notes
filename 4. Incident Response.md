## Module 4: Incident Response & Management


> [!goal] Learning Objectives
> *   Understand and apply the [[NIST SP 800-61]] incident response lifecycle.
> *   Define the role of a CSIRT.
> *   Create and follow incident response playbooks.

---


### 4.1 Incident Response Frameworks

Structured approaches are vital for a calm and effective response. The most common is **[[NIST SP 800-61]]**, which outlines a four-step process:

1.  **Preparation:** Making the organization ready for an incident.
2.  **Detection & Analysis:** Identifying and investigating potential incidents.
3.  **Containment, Eradication & Recovery:** Stopping the incident, removing the threat, and restoring systems.
4.  **Post-Incident Activity:** Learning from the incident to improve future response.

### 4.2 The CSIRT

The **Computer Security Incident Response Team (CSIRT)** is a cross-functional group responsible for managing the response to a security incident. It typically includes members from security, IT, legal, PR, and management.

### 4.3 Playbooks and Runbooks

These are pre-defined, step-by-step procedures for handling specific types of incidents (e.g., ransomware, phishing). They ensure a consistent, efficient, and repeatable response, minimizing errors made under pressure.

> [!tip] A good playbook includes:
> *   Triggers for the playbook.
> *   Required tools and access.
> *   Step-by-step technical actions.
> *   Communication guidelines.
> *   Criteria for escalation.
